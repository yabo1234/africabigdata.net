Built on shared values of democracy, human rights, and sustainable development, this partnership addresses global challenges through coordinated action across multiple domains.

Five Key Transformation Areas

The Global Gateway focuses on five strategic areas to accelerate Africa's sustainable transformation:

🌱 Accelerating the Green Transition

To tackle the global challenge of climate change, the EU works with Africa to maximise the benefits of a job-rich green transition and minimise threats to the environment in full compliance with the Paris Agreement.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Sustainable Energy: Increase renewable energy and hydrogen share in the energy mix
  • Biodiversity: Sustainable use of natural resources and nature-based solutions
  • Agri-food Systems: More sustainable African agri-food systems
  • Climate Resilience: Disaster risk reduction and climate adaptation
2030 Ambition: Increase renewable energy generation capacity by at least an additional 300 GW, improve livelihood of 65 million people, and stabilise 3 million km² of land.

Spotlight: Clean Hydrogen Production

Massive deployment of renewable energy and clean hydrogen production across Africa would contribute to at least 40 Gigawatts of electrolyser capacity by 2030, unlocking business opportunities in energy intensive industries.

💻 Accelerating the Digital Transition

Tackling the global digital divide and strengthening secure digital connections between Europe and Africa, and across Africa, is a common priority enhanced by the post-COVID-19 digital imperative.

Key Initiatives:

  • Digital Infrastructure: Submarine and terrestrial fibre-optic cables
  • Cloud & Data: Cloud and data infrastructures development
  • Regulatory Frameworks: Supporting digital transition governance
  • AU-EU Digital4Development Hub: Coordinated digital development
2030 Ambition: Accelerate universal access for all in Africa to reliable internet networks safely and securely wherever they live.

Spotlight: Major Cable Projects

  • Medusa Flagship: Underwater cabling connecting Northern Africa to EU
  • Africa 1 Cable: Linking Europe to the entire East coast of Africa
  • West Africa Extension: First landing planned in Dakar, Senegal

📈 Accelerating Sustainable Growth and Job Creation

Substantially increasing investments to boost regional and continental economic integration, growth and decent job creation across multiple sectors.

Key Areas:

  • Transport: Strategic corridors and multimodal transport networks
  • Business Support: Early-stage businesses and young entrepreneurs
  • Economic Integration: Supporting AfCFTA implementation
  • Raw Materials: Sustainable mineral value chains
  • Science & Innovation: Africa-EU Science, Technology and Innovation Initiative
2030 Ambition: Integrate African and European multimodal transport networks, increase private sector contribution to Africa's growth, and strengthen continental economic integration.

AfCFTA Support

Additional €630 million announced to support AfCFTA operationalisation, including €24.2 million Technical Assistance Facility co-funded by France, Germany and Sweden.

🏥 Strengthening Health and Pharmaceutical Systems

Building resilient health systems and supporting successful vaccination campaigns to mitigate COVID-19 effects and provide better healthcare for the future.

Focus Areas:

  • Vaccine Infrastructure: Deployment, infrastructure and production capacities
  • Skills Development: Healthcare workforce strengthening
  • Regulatory Frameworks: Pharmaceutical system governance
  • Universal Health Coverage: Accessible healthcare systems
2030 Ambition: Improved vaccination coverage and strengthened African pharmaceutical system with regional manufacturing capacities to meet local needs and demand.

Manufacturing and Access Initiative

Team Europe initiative supporting African partners in strengthening local pharmaceutical systems, including support for the African Medicines Agency (AMA) and WHO technology transfer hub.

🎓 Investing in Education and Training

Supporting African partners in providing modern, quality education and training for all children and youth, addressing the unprecedented learning disruption caused by COVID-19.

Key Components:

  • Quality Education: Modern education systems for all children and youth
  • Skills Training: Training corresponding to labour market opportunities
  • Youth Mobility: Facilitating educational exchange
  • Teacher Development: Enhancing teachers' competences and skills
2030 Ambition: Expanded skills development opportunities to meet emerging needs for Africa's economic and social transformation.

Youth Mobility for Africa

Flagship initiative promoting learning mobility opportunities within Africa and between Africa and the EU, supporting cooperation in higher education and promoting Africa as a study destination.

Financing the Global Gateway

The €150 billion investment package combines multiple financing mechanisms:

💰 Grants and Loans

EU and Member States' bilateral aid, combining grants and loans while attracting private funding from both Africa and Europe through blended finance and guarantees.

🌱 Global Green Bond Initiative

Supporting partner countries in developing and scaling up green bond markets to attract greater financing from international capital markets and institutional investors.

🏦 Special Drawing Rights

€100 million grant agreement for IMF's Poverty Reduction and Growth Trust, enabling €630 million worth of zero interest loans for eligible African countries.

🌱 Green Transition

Focus Areas:

  • Sustainable agriculture and food security
  • Biodiversity conservation and ecosystem protection

💻 Digital Economy

Focus Areas:

Historical Timeline

1957-1975: Early Foundations

Initial cooperation through the Yaoundé Conventions and Lomé Conventions, establishing trade and development partnerships between European countries and African states.

2000: Cotonou Agreement

The Cotonou Partnership Agreement established a comprehensive framework for cooperation between the EU and African, Caribbean, and Pacific (ACP) countries.

2007: First EU-Africa Summit

The inaugural EU-Africa Summit in Lisbon marked the beginning of formal EU-AU institutional dialogue and strategic partnership.

2010: Second EU-Africa Summit

Held in Tripoli, this summit focused on investment, economic growth, and integration, strengthening the partnership framework.

2014: Fourth EU-Africa Summit

The Brussels summit emphasized peace, security, and sustainable development, establishing new cooperation mechanisms.

2017: Fifth EU-Africa Summit

The Abidjan summit focused on youth, mobility, and investment, launching new initiatives for economic partnership.

2022: Sixth EU-AU Summit

The Brussels summit established the current six-pillar framework and committed to a renewed partnership for the 21st century.

2022-Present: Global Gateway Launch

Launch of the €150 billion Global Gateway Africa-Europe Investment Package, representing the largest investment commitment in EU-Africa relations.

2023-Present: Implementation Phase

Ongoing implementation of the six-pillar framework and Global Gateway initiatives with regular monitoring and evaluation.

Current Initiatives

  • Global Gateway Africa: EU's €150 billion investment strategy for sustainable infrastructure development
  • Team Europe Initiatives: Coordinated EU member state and institution responses to African development priorities
  • AU-EU Innovation Agenda: Joint research and innovation programmes focusing on African priorities
  • Digital for Development (D4D) Hub: Supporting Africa's digital transformation through technology transfer
  • African Peace Facility: EU funding for AU-led peace support operations
  • Pan-African Programme: Supporting continental integration and AU institutional capacity

Future Outlook

  • Deepened economic integration through trade and investment
  • Joint responses to climate change and environmental challenges
  • Collaborative approaches to migration and mobility
  • Shared leadership on international peace and security
  • Innovation partnerships in technology and digital transformation
